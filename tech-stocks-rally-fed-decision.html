Tech Stocks Rally as Federal Reserve Signals Rate Pause
By Sarah Johnson | December 11, 2024 | Markets & Economy

Major technology stocks surged Wednesday following the Federal Reserve's announcement that it would pause interest rate hikes for the remainder of the quarter, citing improved inflation metrics and stabilizing economic indicators.

Apple Inc. (AAPL) led the rally, climbing 3.2% to close at $195.45, while Microsoft Corporation (MSFT) gained 2.8% to reach $378.90. The tech-heavy NASDAQ Composite Index jumped 1.9%, its strongest single-day performance in three weeks.

Market Movers:
• AAPL: +3.2% ($195.45)
• MSFT: +2.8% ($378.90)
• GOOGL: +2.1% ($142.30)
• NVDA: +4.5% ($495.20)

"This decision reflects the Fed's confidence that inflation is moving toward our 2% target," said Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell during the press conference. "We believe the economy is in a good position to weather current challenges without additional monetary tightening."

The pause in rate hikes removes a significant headwind for growth stocks, particularly in the technology sector where higher borrowing costs have pressured valuations throughout 2024.

Investment analysts view the Fed's decision as a positive signal for equity markets heading into the final weeks of the year. Goldman Sachs raised its year-end S&P 500 target to 4,800, citing improved sentiment and strong corporate earnings.

Treasury yields fell sharply on the news, with the 10-year note dropping 12 basis points to 4.18%. The dollar weakened against major currencies as rate differential expectations narrowed.

Looking ahead, market participants will focus on upcoming inflation data and the Fed's December meeting minutes for further guidance on monetary policy trajectory in early 2025.
